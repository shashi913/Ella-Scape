Ravana Falls

History and Ledgend of Ravana Falls

The legend behind Ravana Falls is based on the Hindu epic, Ramayana. According to the legend, Ravana, the king of Lanka, kidnapped Sita, the wife of Lord Rama, and took her to his kingdom. Lord Rama, along with his army of monkeys and bears, went on a mission to rescue Sita and defeat Ravana.

During the final battle between Rama and Ravana, Ravana was killed by Rama's arrows. However, before he died, Ravana instructed his brother Vibhishana to perform the last rites on his body. Vibhishana, who had joined Rama's army, was torn between his loyalty to his brother and his loyalty to Lord Rama.

To resolve Vibhishana's dilemma, Lord Rama asked Hanuman, the monkey god, to find a suitable place to perform the last rites. Hanuman flew to Sri Lanka and found several waterfalls in the area. One of these waterfalls was Ravana Falls, which was located in a cave behind the waterfall. Hanuman suggested that this was the ideal place to perform the last rites, as the water from the falls would wash away Ravana's sins.

After Vibhishana performed the last rites at Ravana Falls, Lord Rama blessed the waterfall and said that anyone who bathed in its waters would be cleansed of their sins. This legend has made Ravana Falls a popular destination for both tourists and devotees.

The Natural Beauty of Ravana Falls

Ravana Falls is a stunningly beautiful waterfall located in the hills of Sri Lanka. The waterfall cascades down from a height of about 25 meters (82 feet) and is surrounded by lush green forests and hills, making it a picturesque spot that is a feast for the eyes.

The area around the waterfall is rich in biodiversity, with various species of plants and animals found in the vicinity. The lush greenery around the waterfall, along with the cool mist that rises from the water, creates a refreshing and tranquil atmosphere that is ideal for relaxing and unwinding.

Activities in Ravana Falls

One of the best activities to do in Ravana Falls is to simply take in the stunning scenery. The waterfall is surrounded by lush green forests and hills, making it a picturesque spot that is a feast for the eyes. You can relax on the rocks by the waterfall and take in the tranquil atmosphere.

Visitors can also take a dip in the cool waters of the waterfall, which is a popular activity during hot summer days. The sound of the cascading water and the sight of the mist rising from the falls is a soothing and refreshing experience for visitors.

In addition to the waterfall, the surrounding landscape offers breathtaking views of the hills and forests that are a treat for nature lovers and photographers. Overall, the natural beauty of Ravana Falls Ella is truly awe-inspiring and leaves a lasting impression on anyone who visits it.

The Biodiversity of Ravana Falls

Ravana Falls Ella is not only a beautiful waterfall but also a biodiversity hotspot in Sri Lanka. The area surrounding the waterfall is rich in flora and fauna, with several species of plants, animals, and birds thriving in the lush green forests and hills.

Flora

The area around Ravana Falls is home to a diverse range of plants, including several endemic species. The forests around the waterfall are dominated by tropical rainforest trees such as Dipterocarpus zeylanicus, Mesua ferrea, and Hopea sp. The forest understory is covered with a thick layer of shrubs, ferns, and creepers. There are also several species of orchids, including the endemic Bulbophyllum macraei, growing in the area.

Fauna

Ravana Falls is home to several species of animals, including mammals, reptiles, and insects. Some of the notable mammal species found in the area include the purple-faced langur, toque macaque, Indian palm squirrel, and giant squirrel. Reptile species such as the Sri Lankan green pit viper, common krait, and various species of frogs are also found in the area.

Ravana Falls is a birdwatcher's paradise, with several endemic and migratory bird species present in the area. Some of the notable bird species found in the area include the Sri Lankan junglefowl, black-capped bulbul, Sri Lanka blue magpie, and Sri Lanka white-eye. The nearby forest reserve is also home to several endemic bird species such as the Sri Lanka hanging parrot and Sri Lanka spur fowl.
